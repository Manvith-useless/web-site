# Game Design Document — Cameleon: Dual Fate

## Elevator Pitch
Cinematic third-person action-adventure where a shape-shifting assassin awakens a conscience and confronts a corrupted friend amid a shadow war between parallel Earths.

## Core Pillars
- Identity and deception: seamless shape-shifting, social stealth, and consequences
- Weighty, expressive combat: Echo Blades combos, grapples, and stealth finishers
- Dual campaigns and moral choice: branching missions, unique bosses, multiple endings
- Co-op synergy: complementary abilities and combo interplay (Redemption/Corruption)
- Cinematic immersion: grounded modern settings with surreal Meridan anomalies

## Playable Characters
- Cameleon: illegal Meridan awakener; shape-shift into any human form; Echo Blades
- Zero: weapon savant; instantly masters any weapon; precise counters, stance swaps
- Devil: pact with Greed; consumes matter to energy; brutal brawler/caster hybrid
- Eclipse: shadow manipulator; illusions, clones, light-suppression arenas

## Signature Mechanics
- Shape-shift: scan, assume, and maintain disguises; suspicion and tells
- Echo Blades: chained blades + serrated knife; grapple, pull, AoE whips, stealth kills
- Consume (Devil): convert objects/enemies to energy; overheat and corruption risk
- Weapon Mastery (Zero): instant proficiency trees; stance/weapon-contextual specials
- Morality System: actions alter faction trust, endings, and mission paths
- Co-op Links: duo takedowns, combo tags, revive loops, shared objectives

## Campaign Structure
- Redemption Campaign (Cameleon + Zero)
  - Infiltration, protection, and exposure of Greed’s network
  - Boss: Cameleon vs Devil in a collapsing Meridan-flooded city
- Corruption Campaign (Devil + Eclipse)
  - Sabotage, coercion, and consolidation of Greed’s influence
  - Boss: Zero vs Eclipse in a shifting illusion chamber

## Progression
- Ability nodes gated by narrative beats and Meridan artifacts
- Echo Blade mods, stealth tools, and co-op link upgrades
- Reputation tracks: agencies, syndicates, and civilian trust

## World & Tone
- Modern-day cities with Meridan fractures; grounded tech meets cosmic anomalies
- GTA V-level visual fidelity target; HDRP; cinematic cameras and VFX-heavy bosses

## Boss Fights
- City Collapse (Cameleon vs Devil): unstable Meridans cause environmental hazards
- Illusion Chamber (Zero vs Eclipse): shifting geometry, fake tells, shadow clones

## Endings
- Multiple based on choices, campaign, and morality thresholds; epilogues per faction

## Accessibility & UX
- Remappable inputs, color-blind safe VFX, camera comfort options, generous checkpoints

## Monetization
- Premium single purchase; optional cosmetic-only DLC (campaign epilogues, skins)
